About Shared Stories

In a world that often feels disconnected, Shared Stories exists as a digital sanctuary where authentic human experiences can be shared, heard, and honored. We believe that every person carries stories worth telling, and that by sharing these stories, we discover our shared humanity.

This platform was born from a simple observation: people crave genuine connection. Not the performative kind that dominates social media, but the vulnerable, honest, imperfect kind that makes us feel less alone. We wanted to create a space that feels less like a website and more like a trusted journal‚Äîwarm, safe, and inherently human.

Design Philosophy: Creating a Safe Emotional Space

Every visual choice on this platform was made intentionally to support the emotional experience of sharing and reading stories. This isn't just about aesthetics‚Äîit's about creating an environment that feels inherently safe and welcoming.

üé≠ The Red Hat Approach (Emotional Intelligence)

This platform was designed through the lens of the Red Hat from Edward de Bono's Six Thinking Hats method‚Äîprioritizing emotional awareness, intuition, and feelings. The Red Hat asks: "How does this make people feel?" Every design decision answers that question.

Design Elements That Foster Safety

  • Warm, Soft Color Palette: Creams, peaches, and sage greens evoke comfort rather than clinical perfection
  • Readable Typography: Serif headings (Lora) feel personal and handwritten, while body text (Open Sans) ensures clarity
  • Generous White Space: Stories need room to breathe, and so do readers
  • Gentle Interactions: Soft shadows, smooth transitions, and subtle animations never feel jarring
  • Mood Indicators: Visual cues help readers emotionally prepare for the story they're about to read
  • No Performative Metrics: No view counts, likes, or engagement metrics‚Äîjust stories existing for their own sake

Acknowledging Risks & Our Responses

We believe in transparency about the challenges and responsibilities of running a platform centered on vulnerable storytelling. Here's what we've considered:

Privacy Concerns

Risk: Sharing personal stories online always carries privacy risks, even with anonymity options.
Our Response: We emphasize optional anonymity, allow story removal at any time, and never require identifying information beyond a private email for contact purposes.

Emotional Safety

Risk: Reading or sharing difficult stories can be emotionally triggering.
Our Response: We use mood indicators, gentle language throughout, and encourage readers to prioritize their emotional well-being. We're also transparent that we're not a substitute for professional mental health support.

Content Moderation

Risk: Without careful moderation, any platform can become a space for harmful content.
Our Response: All stories are reviewed before publication. We have clear community guidelines that prioritize respect, authenticity, and safety. We reserve the right to decline content that doesn't align with our values.

User Hesitation

Risk: People may feel hesitant to share, fearing judgment or vulnerability.
Our Response: The entire design is built to lower barriers‚Äîfrom reassuring microcopy to anonymous options to a journal-like aesthetic that feels private even while being shared.

Spam & Bad Faith Submissions

Risk: Open submission forms can attract spam or insincere content.
Our Response: Human review of all submissions ensures quality and authenticity. We prioritize genuine storytelling over quantity.

About This Project

Shared Stories is a semester project created to explore how design can facilitate authentic human connection in digital spaces. This project examines the intersection of storytelling, emotional design, and ethical technology.

The site demonstrates how thoughtful design choices‚Äîfrom typography to color to interaction patterns‚Äîcan create environments that feel emotionally safe. It's a study in designing for empathy, vulnerability, and the complex emotional landscape of sharing personal narratives online.

Designed and developed as an exploration of human-centered design principles, Six Thinking Hats methodology (Red Hat emphasis), and the responsibility that comes with creating platforms for vulnerable expression.
